About

ROOSTER stands for Robust Object Oriented Solver of Transport Equations in a Reactor.

The code has been developed since early 2021 as a platform for accumulating and disseminating experience in the field of a Sodium Fast Reactor.

ROOSTER
  • is open source, multiphysics and object-oriented;
  • is written in Python 3 with Fortran 2009 math kernels;
  • aims at exceptional transparency and readability to facilitate both contributing from experts and understanding for new users;
  • uses composable architecture by class instantiation and customisation inheritance;
  • applies threading and MPI for multi-processor multiple-node execution; and
  • relies on a structured mesh allowing an acceleration compared to unstructured meshes due to easy identification of elements and nodes.

The structured mesh feature makes a main difference of ROOSTER from other modern multi-physics nuclear reactor modeling tools, which usually use unstructured meshes.
